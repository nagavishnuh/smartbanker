How It Works?


To understand the need and purpose of our website, let us go through a small story of two friends who wish to deposit their money in bank. Let's see how they end up at last :

Jay and Sophie are close friends from childhood have grown up and are well settled in their respective fields. Both decide to open a saving account; Jay on one hand opens saving account in well-known corporate bank which is guided by the government and has all privacy related issues covered. On the other hand, Sophie gets attracted towards false

advertisements (generally they tempt customers by providing high interest rates on deposits and low interest rates on loans) of a fairly new bank. She starts depositing

her savings and feels that her money is safe, then after a year the bank faces crises leading to bankruptcy and eventually the downfall of the bank. When Sophie gets to know about the bank in which she deposits her savings, feels vulnerable of losing her money & is in a clueless position. On the other hand, Jay sees that his bank gives him proper interest at

correct times. This helps Jay to lead a planned future. Also he helps Sophie further to overcome her crisis. Not everyone will be so kind like Jay right! Hence this shows

that, making the right choices by being aware of the bank situation, plays a vital in trusting it. Being tempted and trusting a bank by its false traps can push our money to higher risk in the long run. Hence to overcome this tough situation, we help you to know how much you can rely on a particular bank and also we try to address the point that for how long you can rely on a particular bank. So, get smart at banking

with The Smart Banker , and please don’t be a Sophie.
NOTE: Please read the below tips to get an idea about what to do next.

Tips:

  • For any information about the parameter, just hover on its label.
  • Know about the past history, performance of any bank that you intend to keep your money.
  • Don’t get into trap of falling to the advertisements/exclusive benefits that are said initially to attract customers for trusting in a bank.
  • Make wise decisions (as per the needs) before opening a saving account from a pool of banks/co-operatives.
